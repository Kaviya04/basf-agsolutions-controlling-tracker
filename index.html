Financial Performance & Controlling Review

An operational read on eighteen years of BASF SE reported financials — built the way a controlling analyst would frame it: trend, bridge, and balance sheet strength, with commentary on what moved and why.

Prepared by Kaviya Gopal — for the START IN Finance & Controlling Trainee Programme application
01

Revenue & margin trend

EUR million · FY2008–FY2025
Reading Revenue has been range-bound between roughly €57–87bn for a decade, but the shape of the last four years is the story: FY2022's €87.3bn peak — inflated by post-pandemic price pass-through and energy cost recovery — gave way to a 32% decline to €59.7bn by FY2025, as European chemical demand softened and pricing normalized. EBIT margin tells a more encouraging second half: after bottoming near zero in FY2020 and again compressing to 2.8–3.3% through the FY2022–24 downturn, it recovered to 4.3% in FY2025 — margin expansion arriving despite falling revenue, which only happens through cost discipline, not volume recovery.
02

FY2025 P&L bridge

EUR million · vs. FY2024, line items as reported
Reading Revenue fell €5.6bn (-8.6%) year-on-year, and gross profit followed it down by €2.8bn — cost of revenue did not scale down proportionally with sales, which is typical when fixed manufacturing costs sit under a shrinking top line. The offset came below the gross profit line: SG&A was cut by €1.4bn, roughly 14%, enough on its own to outweigh over half the gross profit decline. Combined with a smaller R&D reduction, operating expense discipline is the reason EBIT rose €0.5bn to €2.56bn even as revenue fell — a controlling-driven result, not a demand-driven one.
03

Balance sheet strength

Equity ratio & leverage, FY2008–FY2025
Reading The equity ratio held in the healthy 44–49% range through the FY2021–22 peak, but has eroded for three straight years to 44.0% in FY2025 as retained earnings absorbed weaker net income while the balance sheet didn't shrink as fast as equity did. Net debt has climbed steadily since FY2021 — from €16.5bn to €21.8bn — while EBITDA compressed, pushing net debt/EBITDA from 1.5x to 3.5x. That's the leverage-side counterpart to the cost story above: SG&A cuts are protecting EBIT margin, but the balance sheet is carrying more debt against a smaller earnings base, which is the kind of tension a controlling function exists to flag and manage — not just report.
Source: BASF SE standalone financial statements (income statement & balance sheet), FY2008–FY2025, EUR.
Methodology: all figures derived directly from reported line items — no estimates or restatements. Ratios computed as: Equity ratio = Total Equity / Total Assets; Net debt = Short-term + Long-term debt − Cash; Net debt/EBITDA = Net debt / EBITDA.
